This incident, while global in scale, heavily impacted users in the Philippines, revealing vulnerabilities in data privacy and the ethical responsibilities of technology companies. Case Study Analysis

An in-depth analysis of the Facebook-Cambridge Analytica data breach, exploring ethical issues, applying theoretical frameworks, and proposing solutions grounded in IT ethics and the Data Privacy Act.

Incident Summary: The Facebook-Cambridge Analytica data scandal of 2018 was a pivotal incident that exposed vulnerabilities in data privacy, particularly impacting Filipino users. Unauthorized data collection via a third-party app violated informational privacy, obscured consent, and eroded trust. The data was used for targeted political advertising, raising significant ethical concerns.

Ethical Issues Identified:

  • Breach of Informational Privacy: Unauthorized collection and processing of personal data violated the right to control one's information.
  • Lack of Valid Consent: Data was processed without freely given, specific, informed consent, violating the Data Privacy Act's principles.
  • Violation of Trust and Professional Responsibility: Both Facebook and Cambridge Analytica failed to uphold moral responsibilities to protect user data.
  • Misrepresentation and Deception: The purpose of data collection was obscured, bordering on deception.

Proposed Solutions:

  • Strengthened Consent Mechanisms: Implement clear, granular consent options to empower users.
  • Enhanced Transparency: Ensure users can access, rectify, or erase their data.
  • Stricter Third-Party Controls: Vet app developers and monitor data access.
  • Data Minimization: Collect only necessary data for legitimate purposes.
  • Robust Security: Implement safeguards and prompt breach notifications.
  • Professional Ethics: Adhere to codes ensuring responsible data handling.
